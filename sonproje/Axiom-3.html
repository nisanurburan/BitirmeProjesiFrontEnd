They will conduct many scientific experiments
Turkey's first astronaut Alper Gezeravcı will conduct 13 experiments on the International Space Station for 14 days. These experiments will be in areas such as human genetics, human health and biology in microgravity and space environments. In the new generation alloys experiment for space, studies will be carried out on the production of alloys resistant to high temperatures. The experiment is aimed to contribute to the development of materials for the space, aviation and defense industries. Growth tests of single-celled organisms that have adapted to harsh conditions on Earth in zero-gravity conditions will also be carried out.

Changes in plants will be examined
The changes of plants under microgravity will be examined. The effect of propolis substance on bacteria in microgravity will be investigated. Another experiment will be to detect disturbances due to sound frequency changes. With the support of artificial intelligence, the oxygen level of the air will be calculated and the disorders caused by low gravity will be identified. The studies will be carried out in coordination with TUBITAK Space, Axiom Space and NASA.

Gene research experiments will also be conducted
The second experiment that Gezeravcı will conduct will be the investigation of CRISPR-GEM gene editing efficiency in plants under microgravity, where Tuğçe Celayir from Yıldız Technical University is the project manager. The aim of the experiment in question is to investigate the effectiveness of CRISPR, one of the modern gene editing techniques of molecular biology, on plants in a microgravity environment, aimed at understanding and improving the defense mechanisms of plants, which are the skeleton of bioregenerative life support systems, against biological and non-biological stresses that occur during space missions. Gezeravcı started his experiments with "EXTREMOPHYTE", which investigated the physiological and molecular responses of the endemic halophyte plant named Schrenkiella Parvula, which grows in Salt Lake, to the space environment.
